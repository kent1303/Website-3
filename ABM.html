What is Agent-Based Modeling (ABM)?

Agent-Based Modeling (ABM) is a simulation modeling technique used to study the interactions of autonomous agents, such as individuals, groups, or organizations, within a specific environment. ABM helps to explore how these interactions lead to the emergence of complex system behaviors.

Key Features of ABM:
  • Agents operate independently, following specific rules and behaviors.
  • Agents interact with each other and their environment.
  • The model captures emergent phenomena, revealing patterns and trends.

Why Use ABM?

ABM is valuable for understanding complex systems where individual-level behaviors influence larger system outcomes. It is especially useful when traditional modeling approaches, such as equation-based models, are insufficient for capturing the dynamics of a system.
